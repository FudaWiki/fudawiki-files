_Guru-Guru Onsen 3_ (<span lang="ja">ぐるぐる温泉3</span>) is a video game for the Sega Dreamcast published in 2002 by Sega and developed by Overworks. It is a collection of games, and the first that includes a hanafuda game, namely [Koi-Koi](/en/hanafuda/games/koi-koi). Non-karuta games featured in the video game are mahjong, reversi, [daifugō](https://fudawiki.org/en/karuta/games#japanese-games-using-western-playing-cards), and [Napoleon](https://fudawiki.org/en/karuta/games#japanese-games-using-western-playing-cards). Later in 2002, [Dai Guru-Guru Onsen](/en/hanafuda/video-games/ps2/online-games-dai-guruguru-onsen) was released for the PlayStation 2, which includes all games from Guru-Guru Onsen 2 and Guru-Guru Onsen 3.

The game is remarkable for having a 4-player game mode for Koi-Koi, where 2 teams of 2 players play against each other.

# Gameplay and rules

The Koi-Koi game (just called "hanafuda" in-game) features both a regular 2-player mode and a "2 vs 2" team play mode. All games support both offline play against CPU opponents and online multiplayer, though online play is probably no longer supported since the servers went down.

When playing Koi-Koi, each player or team starts with an amount of points determined before the game (5 to 60 points, 15 by default). At the end of each round, the winning party's yaku score is subtracted from the losing party's game points. The first to reach 0 points loses the game. It is possible to set a maximum number of rounds (6, 9 or 12), but by default there is no limit to the number of rounds.

## Koi-Koi rules
The game uses Koi-Koi rules that are close to the classic Koi-Koi rules that were common in early hanafuda video games.

- The values of Bright yaku are 15, 10, 8, and 6 points (for 5, 4, Rainy 4, and 3 Brights respectively).
- Boar-Deer-Butterflies and the two special Ribbon yaku are 6 points each.  (None of these earn bonus points for extra cards, and each can combine with their respective generic Animal/Ribbon yaku.)
- Blossom Viewing and Moon Viewing are 3 points each.
  - The game settings have the following options for Viewing yaku: Viewing yaku can end the round, Viewing yaku can not end the round (at least one other yaku is required), or Viewing yaku are turned off.
- A Dealer’s Privilege rule is used: in case neither player manages to finish the round before the end, the dealer wins the round with 1 point.
- The special Sake Cup rule is not used: the Sake Cup card only counts as an Animal card.
- No score multipliers of any kind are used.
- The winner becomes the dealer in the next round.

## Koi-Koi team play
In the 2 vs 2 game mode, two teams of two players each play against each other. The rules are the same as the regular Koi-Koi game mode, but with the following additions and changes:
- Each player gets 4 hand cards at the start of each round instead of 8. (The field cards in the middle stay at 8.)
- Captured cards are shared between team members. (Each team has one player who collects all captured cards for their team.)
- If a yaku is made, the team member whose turn it is gets to make the decision whether to call koi-koi, and becomes the dealer if they end the round.

The game settings include an option called "show cards" [札見せ, *fuda mise*], that allows players to show any 2 cards in their hand to their team mate at the start of each round. This option is off by default.
