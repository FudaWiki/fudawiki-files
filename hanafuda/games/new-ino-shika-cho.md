Ino-Shika-Chō - 猪鹿蝶 [いのしかちょう, lit. “boar-deer-butterfly”] is a [Roppyakken](/en/hanafuda/games/roppyakken) variant for two to three players, played in the Hokkaido region of Japan.

The name comes from the Yaku of the same name being the highest scoring Yaku in the game.

Note that the name of this game may also refer to a [simple gambling game](/en/hanafuda/games/ino-shika-cho) with a completely different gameplay style; for this reason, the gambling game is sometimes refered to as "Old Ino-Shika-Chō". 

# Gameplay
Gameplay is identical to Roppyakken, except for some differences:
- There are 2 wild cards, and the rules of using them are different.
- Ribbon cards are worth 5 points instead of 10.
- There are two less Dekiyaku than in Roppyakken; Shiko and Nanatan are not counted in this game. *(For this reason, "Boar, Deer, Butterflies" is the Dekiyaku with the highest value.)*
- There are different rules for "Fuke".

## Wild Cards
The ![monthicon_11.png](/hanafuda/icons/monthicon_11.png =20x)Rain Man card and the ![Icon for month 9](/hanafuda/icons/monthicon_9.png =20x)Sake Cup card function as (limited) wild cards in this game. 

[![Willow with Rain Man](/sheldonchen-cards/110.svg#hanafuda-card =50x)](/en/hanafuda/suits/willow#rain-man)[![Chrysanthemum with Sake Cup](/sheldonchen-cards/90.svg#hanafuda-card =50x)](/en/hanafuda/suits/chrysanthemum#sake-cup)

This means that each one of them, **when drawn from the draw pile**, can be used to match any card **other** than 0-point cards. The exception is when there are ![monthicon_11.png](/hanafuda/icons/monthicon_11.png =20x)Willow or ![Icon for month 9](/hanafuda/icons/monthicon_9.png =20x)Chrysanthemum cards on the field; then it must capture one of them. In addition, they **cannot** be used to capture sets of 3 of any other suit.

If the wild card is **on a player's hand**, it cannot be used as a wild card except when held by the dealer.

If the wild card is **on the field**, then it no longer functions as a wild card, and must be captured by a card of the same month.

## Fuke
- If any player other than the dealer has only scored **60 points** or fewer during a round, all players' score calculations for the round, including card points, Teyaku points, and Dekiyaku points, are invalidated. This negation of scoring is called *fuke* [ふけ, lit. "dandruff"].
- If the **dealer** has only scored **60 points** or fewer during a round, all players' score calculations for the round are invalidated **except for the other two players' Dekiyaku points.**
